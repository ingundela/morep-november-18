Teach with digital technologies


Rather than require these digital familiar students to power down their devices when they walk into the classroom, they need to power them up,leveraging their interest in technology to create meaningful learning experiences that connect students to each other and to the wider world. And administrators and teachers are using digital tools to manage the classroom, assess student progress and administer newly mandated online state testing.

Morep Lda is focusing to provide best possible education solutions to the learners and teachers for a seamless learning environment , through Digital Technology Intervention in the education sector.


As recently as 2000, computers were largely used only to teach computer skills. Today, educators are using technology to transform learning and teaching, creating powerful 1:1 computing environments in which every child has a device, equipped with digital curriculum that allows for self-directed learning. Far from replacing teachers, though, this technology allows educators to explore new models for learning and rethink collaboration and communication in the classroom. The classroom shifting to a student-centric model and students and teachers will use devices to learn together.


priorities:

The transformative potential of technology for education involves a great deal more than simply purchasing a fleet of laptops and making them available in the classroom.



The following three priorities highlight the key elements that instituitions will need to bring together for a successful transition:


1) Personalized learning

One size fits all curriculum directed at the average student for entire class entire class can leave exceptional students bored and struggling students lagging behind. devices equipped with personalized learning apps enable teachers to tailor lessons for students based on their needs and talents, which they may then tackle at their own pace.


2) Digital content and curriculum

Devices when paired with, interactive content and engaging curriculum, encourages students to work in teams to create content, share ideas and solve problems—while also developing the communication and interpersonal skills they’ll need to succeed in their later professional lives.


3) Professional development

Online Resources that train teachers to employ new teaching methods to give teachers the tools toextract the best possible methods from technology to address the new dynamics resulting from assigning work which is more collaborative in nature.

samsung solutions

Samsung Smart School solution uses Samsung Galaxy tablets and large-format classroom displays to create a richer learning environment to empower the new generation of digital natives. In addition our collaboration with partners enables us to offer a broad range of content and mobile apps for rich educational material to enhance learning. Affording peace of mind to schools, learners and parents


STEM (science, technology, engineering, math) employment will continue to increase. STEM Digital lessons also work to close the gender gap between girls and boys in math and science and to create a positive experience to counter unconscious gender bias in schools.


The introduction of mobile STEM labs into schools offers a simple way to integrate digital curriculum into classes on an everyday basis or in specific lessons, including interactive games that incorporate reading.


“The Internet of Things has a role to play as well. For instance, in some countries the local zoos use Samsung School digital solutions to enable students to explore the ecosystem around the zoo. Students use mobile devices and connected peripherals like digital thermometers and microscopes to take pictures, collect samples and send data back to the classroom, where they can work together to create presentations to share with the class. Teachers can then create instant quizzes and polls to quickly assess whether students understand the underlying lesson”.


“The classroom is no longer four walls and a blackboard in the front. Now the whole world is open to students— to use all kinds of digital resources and assets to engage, to collaborate, to learn at their own pace. This really enables them to be true learners.”


“Diane Ashby, National Education Manager, Samsung”

collaboration and communication tools

Digital tools that allow students to work together and to learn and communicate through video, audio, graphics and interactivity help them develop critical thinking and analytical skills as well as the social and cooperative skills they’ll need later in life.


To make the most of these experiences, teachers need technology solutions that combine 1:1 devices with large-format displays and management tools like screen sharing, student screen monitoring and device control.


Students contribute to large screens the content from their own devices. This encourages over all participation, giving rise to interactive dialogue with each other and with teachers. Teachers also have the tools to check student progress on devices and the ability to take control of those devices when necessary.
